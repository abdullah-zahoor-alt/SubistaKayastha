Welcome

I am Subista Kayastha, an interdisciplinary visual artist and researcher.

I work on social issues using creative ideas and learning through experience. My focus is on fixing problems in education by using stories, visuals, and meaningful activities. I work with communities, lead workshops, and create engaging projects that help people think and bring change.

My work explores themes of systemic injustice, alienation, and the human experience through poetic metaphor, visual storytelling, and participatory methods. With a background in expanded design and arts, I create immersive installations and engage with communities to spark dialogue around education, identity, and social structures. I completed my BA (Hons) in Interdisciplinary Expanded Design and Arts at Beaconhouse National University in Lahore, where I was a UNESCO Madanjeet Singh scholar. My thesis focused on alienation within education systems, using symbolic experiences to reconnect audiences with childhood memories and challenge institutional rigidity. One of my recent works, Ephemeral Atmosphere (2024), is a projection-mapped installation that explores fragility, impermanence, and sensory, existential narratives. Outside gallery spaces, I use human-centered design to address real-world issues. I’ve worked on projects like Jugaar and the City (2024), developing portable retail solutions for street vendors, and contributed to archival efforts like the Madan Puraskar Pustakalaya’s Nepali book cover cataloging. My creative toolkit includes illustration, animation, 3D modeling, and projection mapping. Across all my work, I aim to bridge art, research, and activism—always prioritizing ethical collaboration and inclusive storytelling.
